Preamble: The Sovereign State of Gremlantis
We, the Members of Gremlantis, establish this Constitution to ensure order, creativity, fairness, and unity within our virtual nation.
Gremlantis shall be governed by a Head of State, advised by the High Council, guided by the Republic, and safeguarded by a designated Proxy in times of absence.
________________


Article I: Structure of Government
1. Gremlantis is governed by the following bodies:
   * The Head of State 
   * The Proxy to the Head of State
   * The High Council
   * The Republic
2. This Constitution applies to all members of the server.
________________


Article II: The Head of State
1. The Head of State holds ultimate authority over Gremlantis.
2. Responsibilities include:
   * Managing and protecting the server
   * Appointing and removing members of the High Council
   * Appointing or revoking the Proxy
   * Enforcing and interpreting this Constitution
   * Supervising disputes and major decisions
3. The Head of State does not vote on rule creation or amendment, but may provide guidance and interpretation.
4. The Head of State and High Council work together to manage the server and ensure smooth operation.

________________


________________
Article III: The Proxy to the Head of State
   1. The Proxy is a temporary replacement for the Head of State when the Head of State is unable to fulfill their duties.
   2. The Proxy:
   * Must be selected from the High Council
   * May only be appointed or removed by the Head of State
   3. The Proxy assumes the powers of the Head of State only during periods of incapacity or absence.
   4. The Proxy may not:
   * Permanently change the Constitution
   * Remove the Head of State
   * Appoint another Proxy
   * Appoint members to the High Council
________________


________________
Article IV: The High Council
   1. The High Council manages the daily operations of the server alongside the Head of State.
   2. Only users who are proven to be respectful and trustworthy may be appointed to the High Council.
   3. The Head of State will appoint members to the High Council either:
   * From suggestions from existing High Council members
   * Directly by their own discretion
   4. Members of the High Council may be removed by a three-fifths vote from the High Council excluding the member under consideration unless vetoed by the Head of State.
   5. A High Council member who is subject to removal may submit an appeal to the Head of State or Proxy within 48 hours of the vote. The Head of State or Proxy will review the circumstances and issue a final decision.
   6. Responsibilities include:
   * Enforcing server rules and moderating fairly
   * Advising the Head of State
   * Proposing new rules or amendments independently
   * Voting on rule proposals submitted by members of the Republic
   7. Rulemaking process:
   * Rules proposed by the High Council alone are voted on by the High Council.
   * Rules proposed by the Republic are reviewed and voted on by the High Council in the same manner as High Council proposals.
   8. All High Council members are eligible to serve as Proxy, however, selection shall prioritize members who have demonstrated the following:
   * Trustworthiness
   * Respect for the community
   * Responsible service
   9. The High Council shall aim to scale proportionally with membership and activity.
________________


________________
Article V:  The Republic
   1. All non-staff members are Citizens of the Republic of Gremlantis.
   2. Citizens have the right to:
   * Speak and express ideas respectfully
   * Propose rules or amendments for review by the High Council
   * Participate in polls when the High Council chooses to involve them
   3. Citizens must:
   * Follow server rules
   * Respect Staff and other Citizens
   * Contribute positively to the community
________________


Article VI:  Lawmaking and Decisions
   1. Laws and rules may be initiated by:
   * The Head of State
   * The High Council
   * The Republic
   2. Rules initiated by the High Council are voted on solely by the High Council.
   3. Rules proposed by the Republic are reviewed and voted on by the High Council, following the same process.
   4. A rule change may only take effect upon receiving a three-fifths vote including all active members of the High Council.
   5. The Head of State or Proxy oversees enforcement and interpretation but does not vote on rule approval.
________________


Article VII:  Rights of Citizens
   1. All Citizens are guaranteed:
   * Respectful treatment
   * Fair moderation
   * Freedom of expression within server rules
   * The right to propose rules and amendments
   * Participation in community decisions where allowed
________________


________________
Article VIII:  Emergencies and Special Authority
   1. In times of crisis, the Head of State and High Council may take immediate action. Examples of such crises include, but are not limited to:
   * Raids
   * Server threats
   * Major disruptions 
   2. Emergency actions must be reviewed after the crisis ends.
   3. Emergency authority shall not permanently override this Constitution.
________________


Article IX:  Amendments
   1. Amendments may be proposed by:
   * The High Council
   * The Republic
   2. Amendments require:
   * Review and vote by the High Council
   * Enforcement and interpretation by the Head of State
________________


Article X:  Final Authority
   1. This Constitution is the supreme law of Gremlantis.
   2. All rules and decisions must comply with it.
   3. Loyalty is owed first to the Constitution, then to the community of Gremlantis
________________


________________


Article XI: Voting Attendance
   1. All High Council votes shall require the participation of every active High Council member to be considered valid, unless the maximum polling duration has elapsed.
   2. A High Council member may be declared inactive by the Head of State if they have no recorded online presence for seven consecutive days.
   3. Inactive members shall not be counted toward participation or unanimous vote requirements until reinstated.
   4. An inactive member may be reinstated to active status upon notifying the Head of State or Proxy of their return.
   5. All High Council polls shall have a maximum duration of one week, during which the Head of State shall not participate.
   6. A poll shall be declared complete immediately once all active High Council members have cast their votes, even if the one-week duration has not yet elapsed.
   7. Any High Council member may declare themselves inactive for any reason, and may elect not to disclose the reason for their inactivity.
________________


Article XII: Constitutional Protection
   1. All amendments to this constitution, including those proposed by the Head of State, require a unanimous vote from all active High Council members to take effect.
   2. No amendment may be passed without full High Council participation unless a member is formally declared inactive by the Head of State.
   3. The Head of State retains final authority to enforce and interpret all amendments following passage.
   4. Amendments to this constitution that have been voted on by the High Council must be added without any changes to the text contents.
